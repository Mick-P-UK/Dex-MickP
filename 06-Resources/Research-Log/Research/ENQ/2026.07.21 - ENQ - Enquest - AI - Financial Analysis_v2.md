## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis

Enquest's headline FY2025 numbers are almost useless without context, so it is essential to strip the noise from the signal.

- **Turnover** fell 5.3% year-on-year to **$1,118.3m** (2024: $1,180.7m), a decline driven primarily by a 15% year-on-year drop in Brent crude prices rather than volume weakness [2]. Production held up well at 42,945 boepd for the year.
- **Gross profit** dropped 28.6% to **$280.8m** (2024: $393.3m), reflecting the leverage of oil price falls through relatively fixed opex [3].
- **Operating profit** apparently surged to **$648.8m** (2024: $311.5m), but this figure is deeply misleading: it includes a **$391.3m non-cash credit** from settling the Magnus profit-share contingent consideration liability with bp for a $60m cash payment [4]. The underlying **Adjusted EBITDA** actually **fell 25.2% to $503.8m** (2024: $673.9m) [5].
- **PBT** rose sharply to **$493.4m** (2024: $166.6m), again distorted by the Magnus gain [6].
- **Net profit** was virtually **wiped out at $1.6m** (2024: $93.8m, -98.3%), due to a **$491.9m tax charge** that included a **$123.9m non-cash deferred tax hit from the UK EPL extension to March 2030** and a further **$152.4m deferred tax related to the Magnus settlement** [7]. Management's stated underlying post-tax profit stripping out the EPL extension was **$125.5m** [8].
- **EPS** collapsed to **0.1 cents** (2024: 5.0 cents), rendering the headline P/E multiple meaningless. Adjusted EPS was closer to **6.7 cents**.

The takeaway: 2025 was an operationally solid year (production 42.9k boepd, unit opex $25.1/boe, LTIF materially improved to 0.69) that was completely obscured by two one-off items -- the balance-sheet-strengthening but P&L-flattering Magnus settlement, and the accounting hit from the UK's punitive EPL extension.

### Balance Sheet Analysis

- **Net debt rose $48.1m to $433.9m** (Dec 2024: $385.8m), driven by the $20.3m Vietnam cash payment, $17.8m RBL refinancing fees and $15.3m of maiden dividend outflow [1].
- **Cash and equivalents** ended at **$268.8m** (Dec 2024: $280.2m) [2].
- **Gross debt** rose to **$707.5m** ($647.1m bonds plus $60.3m loans) with the $400m RBL facility still fully undrawn -- and now upgraded and extended to an $800m RBL maturing 2031, unlocking $200m of additional liquidity [3]. Cash plus available facilities stood at **$678.6m** at year-end, robust.
- **Shares in issue** unchanged at **1,885.0m** with 20.0m held in Treasury (down from 25.0m); **weighted average diluted shares** of **1,884.1m** for FY2025 EPS purposes (2024: 1,916.2m) [4].
- **Pension obligations** are defined-contribution only and immaterial [5].
- **Decommissioning provisions** rose materially to **$915.6m** plus $15.3m for Thistle (2024: $741.6m plus $18.3m), largely from absorbing the Vietnam $89.1m provision (offset by a $92.1m pre-funded abandonment receivable) plus USD weakness [6].
- **Impairments** were a net non-cash reversal of $5.8m (2024: net charge of $71.4m), consisting of a $94.3m reversal at Kraken partly offset by $88.5m of impairments across Greater Kittiwake, Golden Eagle and Alba [7].

The key balance-sheet story is the **transformational cancellation of the Magnus contingent consideration mechanism**: paying bp $60m upfront removed a $432.9m liability and unlocked c.$777m of future undiscounted cash flows for Enquest.

### Cash Flow Analysis

- **Operating cash flow** dropped 28.5% to **$362.7m** (2024: $507.6m) as lower oil revenues fed through [1].
- **Cash capex** was **$179.2m**, including c.$40m of growth capex on the Seligi 1b gas project [2].
- **Free cash flow** collapsed 83.6% to a wafer-thin **$8.7m** (2024: $53.2m), squeezed by heavy tax and refinancing payments [3].
- **Tax paid** was **$107.2m** of which $104.1m was UK EPL windfall tax in H2 [4].
- **Decommissioning spend** of $56.8m (Heather topsides removal, well P&A campaigns) [5].
- **Refinancing fees** of $17.8m on the RBL upgrade [6].
- **Maiden dividend** cash outflow $15.3m; Vietnam acquisition $20.3m; finance lease payments $83.1m (stepped down from $130.1m as Kraken FPSO contractual terms improved) [7].
- **Opening cash** $280.2m -> **closing cash** $268.8m; **net cash decrease** of $24.5m -- a small net drain despite a challenging year for FCF, which speaks to the group's ability to absorb one-off items.

A prior-year tax adjustment of $19.4m current and $7.8m deferred was recorded in 2025 relating to reclassification of well supplies between inventory and PP&E -- not a formal restatement, but a corrective entry.
- **Board changes**: none disclosed in the provided sources.
- **Principal risks** narrowed from 19 to 11 categories, with the ongoing UK EPL fiscal environment and oil price volatility called out as top-tier concerns [8]. Notably 5.1 MMbbls hedged for the next 12 months at an average floor of **$71.3/bbl** provides useful downside protection.

## Overall Summary and Recommendation

Enquest's headline FY2025 result of $1.6m of net profit is a P&L illusion caused by the deferred tax impact of the UK EPL extension and the accounting treatment of the Magnus settlement. Cut through that noise and you see a solid operational year (43k boepd, unit opex $25.1/boe, HSE metrics materially improved), an **underlying adjusted post-tax profit of c.$125.5m**, and -- critically -- a **transformed balance sheet** with $432.9m of Magnus contingent liability eliminated, an upgraded $800m RBL facility maturing 2031, and $678.6m of liquidity.

The 2026 story is one of active strategic pivot: management is aggressively redeploying capital from the "fiscal outlier" UK North Sea into gas-weighted Southeast Asian assets, headlined by the $833m Malaysia PSC acquisition announced 10 June 2026 that targets 35k boepd of regional net production by 2030. The Bressay gas import, NCP bypass, Malaysia growth, Vietnam extension and Brunei JV together represent one of the largest strategic transformations in the UK-listed E&P space. Add the CEO's stated intent to execute a material UK North Sea consolidation transaction in 2026 (using the $1.85bn UK tax asset and $678m of liquidity), and there is a credible pathway to a materially larger, more diversified, less fiscally-punished Enquest by 2027-28.

The technicals are as constructive as the fundamentals: price is in a powerful, well-supported uptrend, sitting above all major moving averages, with confirming volume, OBV and A/D readings, no bearish divergences and RSI still in the room-to-run zone.

**Recommendation: BUY** (with a special-situation flavour, driven by strategic transformation and cheap headline valuation on the adjusted numbers).

- Immediate upside pivot: a clean break above **27p** opens up **30p+**.
- Suggested technical stop: **24p** (rising 20-day SMA) for a tighter risk profile, or **22p** (May-June consolidation base) for those willing to give the position more room through news volatility.
- Key catalysts to watch: Malaysia PSC completion by December 2026; announced UK North Sea consolidation transaction; H1 2026 results in September 2026; Malaysia DEWA PSC Phase 1 FID targeted for H2 2026; UK fiscal successor to EPL ("OGPM").
