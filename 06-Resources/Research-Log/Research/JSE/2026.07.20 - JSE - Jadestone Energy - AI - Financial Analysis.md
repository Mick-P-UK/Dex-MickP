## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis
- **Turnover trends and growth drivers:** Jadestone achieved record 2025 
production of 19,829 boe/d (a 6% increase year-on-year), generating full-year 
revenues of US$408.1 million [9, 10]. Growth was heavily driven by the Akatara 
field's first full year of production [11, 12]. 2026 production guidance stands
at 18,000–21,000 boe/d, though management expects to hit the lower half of this
range due to weather-related downtime at the Stag field [13, 14].
- **Gross profit and margin analysis:** Adjusted unit operating costs fell an 
impressive 21% to US$28.02/boe in 2025 [9, 15], heavily aided by the lower-cost
production mix introduced by Akatara. Total production costs decreased by 19% 
to US$232.7 million [16, 17].
- **Operating profit performance:** Adjusted EBITDAX increased by 20% to 
US$153.0 million for 2025 [9, 16], reflecting excellent operational cost 
discipline despite a 13% decline in realized Brent oil prices during the year 
[10, 18].
- **PBT and net profit commentary:** Despite strong operational performance, 
the company reported a statutory Loss Before Tax of US$133.7 million and a Net 
Loss After Tax of US$110.7 million [9, 19]. This loss was almost entirely 
driven by a massive non-cash impairment of US$126.0 million pre-tax (US$88.2 
million post-tax) on the Montara and Stag assets, forced by a bearish shift in 
the independent reserves auditor’s forward oil price assumptions [16, 20].
- **EPS implications:** 2025 Basic EPS was negative US$0.20 [9]. For 2026, 
analysts forecast a return to profitability with an EPS of 3.33 US cents [21], 
provided the company escapes further heavy non-cash impairment charges. 

### Balance Sheet Analysis
- **Net debt/cash position:** At the end of 2025, Net Debt was US$89.1 million 
(comprising US$150 million in gross borrowings against US$60.9 million in cash)
[22, 23]. In March 2026, the company successfully executed a highly 
oversubscribed US$200 million Nordic bond issue (12% coupon, maturing 2031) to 
retire its restrictive RBL facility [8, 22]. This significantly bolsters 
near-term liquidity and removes near-term amortization pressures.
- **Share count:** The total number of ordinary shares in issue at the end of 
2025 was 542.16 million (average diluted shares for EPS calculation was 541.15 
million) [24, 25].
- **Pension liabilities:** Pension liabilities are immaterial to the overall 
balance sheet [26]. 
- **Other material items:** Due to the large non-cash impairment, total equity 
slipped into negative territory (-US$78.9 million) by the end of 2025 [27]. 
However, this accounting technicality masks the underlying asset value; the 
independently assessed 2P NPV10 reserve value was US$519 million at the end of 
2025 [28]. 

### Cash Flow Analysis
- **Operating cash flow trends:** Operating cash flow prior to working capital 
movements was highly robust at US$123.6 million in 2025, a 75% increase over 
2024 [9, 15]. 
- **Net cash movement:** Net cash generated from operating activities was 
US$91.4 million [29]. Capital expenditure for the year was US$92.8 million [9],

- **Opening and closing cash balances:** The cash and cash equivalents balance 
fell from US$95.2 million at the start of 2025 to US$60.9 million at year-end

US$117.4 million (including restricted cash) [8].
- **Free cash flow:** Jadestone's 2025-2027 unlevered free cash flow guidance 
remains strong at US$200–US$240 million (assuming US$70/bbl Brent) [14, 31]. 

### Management Commentary and Audit Notes
- **Post-period trading updates:** Q1/Q2 2026 brought a mix of excellent 
strategic milestones and operational headwinds. Management secured the 
long-awaited Field Development Plan (FDP) approval and Gas Sales Agreement for 
the Nam Du/U Minh project in Vietnam, unlocking ~32 MMboe in 2P reserves and 
triggering a farm-out process [8, 32]. Concurrently, the PM323 infill drilling 
campaign in Malaysia is exceeding expectations, with the first two wells adding
~6,000 bopd combined [33, 34].
- **Material risks identified:** Cyclone Narelle inflicted severe damage to the
Stag field's CALM buoy in March 2026. The field is shut-in and not expected to 
return to production until Q4 2026 [8, 13]. While physical and business 
interruption insurance is in place, this introduces operational lag [8]. 
- **Going concern statements:** The Directors have tested the base and downside
cases (including Stag downtime) and are satisfied that the Group has sufficient
financial resources and covenant compliance to continue operations for at least
the next 12 months [35, 36].
