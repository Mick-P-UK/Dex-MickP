## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis

- **Turnover trends and growth drivers:** UiPath reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of
  $418.382 million, an increase of 17% year-over-year (Q1 FY2026: $356.624 million).
  Annualised Recurring Revenue (ARR) grew 12% to $1.901 billion, with net new ARR of
  $49 million and a dollar-based net retention rate of 109%. Growth is driven by
  subscription services, which accounted for $252.903 million in the quarter.
  Management notes that customers are moving from pilot to production with agentic AI
  products, standardising on UiPath as the orchestration and automation execution
  layer for enterprise AI transformation.

- **Gross profit and margin analysis:** Q1 GAAP gross profit was $341.454 million, a
  GAAP gross margin of 82% (Q1 FY2026: also 82%). Non-GAAP gross margin was 83%,
  down from 84% in the prior-year quarter.

- **Operating profit performance:** The company reached a significant milestone in Q1
  FY2027 by reporting its first-ever first-quarter GAAP operating profit of $27.987
  million, against a $16.412 million operating loss in the prior-year period.
  Non-GAAP operating income was $92.491 million, a 22% margin (prior year: $69.616
  million, 20%). Management projects full-year non-GAAP operating income of
  approximately $430 million.

- **Pre-tax income and net profit:** Q1 FY2027 pre-tax income was $40.968 million,
  producing GAAP net income of $22.525 million, a turnaround from the $22.555 million
  net loss in Q1 FY2026. Note the effective tax rate of 45.0%, well above the US
  federal statutory rate, driven by US tax on foreign earnings and rate differences
  between jurisdictions. The provision for income taxes was $18.443 million, taking
  nearly half of pre-tax income.

- **EPS implications:** GAAP diluted EPS for Q1 was $0.04; non-GAAP diluted EPS was
  $0.15. ShareScope estimates full-year FY2027 EPS of $0.785 (78.5 cents).

### Balance Sheet Analysis

- **Net cash position:** UiPath carries zero debt. As of 30 April 2026 it held
  $632.195 million in cash and cash equivalents, $675.049 million in current
  marketable securities and $108.502 million in non-current marketable securities.
  Total cash and marketable securities is therefore **$1.4157 billion**, which is the
  figure UiPath quotes in its own earnings release ("$1.42 billion"). Against 527.818
  million diluted shares that is approximately **$2.68 per share** in highly liquid
  assets.

  *Correction note: v1.0 stated $1.31 billion and $2.48 per share. That figure
  omitted the $108.5 million of non-current marketable securities. The arithmetic was
  sound but the definition differed from the company's own, and was presented as
  though it were the company's.*

- **Share count:** Weighted-average basic share count for Q1 was 523.584 million,
  with 527.818 million diluted shares. The basic count has fallen from 548.451
  million a year earlier, reflecting sustained buyback activity.

- **Share repurchase programme, correct chronology:** Per the 10-Q Stockholders'
  Equity note, the board approved a $500.0 million repurchase authorisation on 1
  September 2023 and a further $500.0 million on 30 August 2024. Those two
  authorisations, $1.0 billion combined, "were fulfilled during the three months
  ended April 30, 2026". Separately, on 5 March 2026 the board authorised an
  additional $500.0 million, disclosed in the Form 8-K filed 11 March 2026. The
  current authorisation has no specified expiration date and may be suspended or
  discontinued at any time.

  In Q1 FY2027 the company repurchased 20.4 million shares at an average $11.47
  (inclusive of brokerage commission), a cash outflow of $243.796 million. Between 1
  and 15 May 2026 it repurchased a further 2.4 million shares at an average $9.63.

  *Correction note: v1.0 asserted that the board authorised the new $500 million
  "immediately after completing a $1 billion program", and read this as a signal of
  confidence. The sequence is the reverse. The $500 million was authorised on 5 March
  2026, BEFORE the earlier $1.0 billion was exhausted later in the same quarter. This
  is a routine top-up of a programme running low, not a post-completion vote of
  confidence.*

- **Insider selling, omitted from v1.0:** The Form 8-K filed 28 May 2026 discloses at
  Item 8.01 that IceVulcan Investments Ltd., an entity controlled by Daniel Dines,
  founder, CEO and Chairman, adopted a Rule 10b5-1 trading plan on 15 April 2026 to
  sell **up to 2,975,000 shares** of Class A common stock through 14 October 2026,
  subject to limit prices. The company states the shares represent less than 5% of Mr
  Dines's holdings, that he entered the plan for tax, asset diversification and
  liquidity reasons, and that he remains a significant controlling stockholder.

  *Correction note: v1.0 cited this exact filing as support for the buyback claim.
  The filing contains no buyback. It contains the selling plan above. A report
  inferring management confidence from repurchases, while omitting the founder-CEO's
  concurrent selling plan disclosed in the same document, is not balanced. Both facts
  belong in the record. Note also that buybacks are a corporate capital-allocation
  decision and insider sales are personal financial decisions; they are not strictly
  contradictory, but a reader is entitled to see both.*

- **Pension liabilities:** No material pension liabilities are reported.

- **Other material items:** Total assets $2.9047 billion against total liabilities
  $1.0017 billion, giving stockholders' equity of $1.903 billion. Accumulated deficit
  is $1.683 billion. Treasury stock stands at $1.0696 billion, up from $833.905
  million at 31 January 2026, consistent with the quarter's repurchases. Goodwill
  rose to $185.695 million and intangibles to $100.120 million following $149.403
  million of business acquisitions in the quarter.

### Cash Flow Analysis

- **Operating cash flow:** Q1 FY2027 net cash from operating activities was $131.928
  million, up from $119.002 million in Q1 FY2026.

- **Net cash movement:** Cash, cash equivalents and restricted cash fell $237.925
  million over the quarter. The two largest drivers were $243.796 million of Class A
  share repurchases and $149.403 million of acquisition payments, partly offset by
  operating inflows and net maturities of marketable securities.

- **Opening and closing balances:** The period opened at $871.595 million and closed
  at $633.670 million in cash, cash equivalents and restricted cash, excluding
  marketable securities.

- **Free cash flow:** Non-GAAP adjusted free cash flow for Q1 was $130.268 million
  (prior year: $117.067 million). Management expects full-year adjusted free cash
  flow of approximately $425 million.

### Management Commentary and Audit Notes

- **Guidance:** Management guides full-year FY2027 revenue of $1.776 billion to
  $1.781 billion, and Q2 FY2027 revenue of $395 million to $400 million with non-GAAP
  operating income of approximately $75 million. Full-year ARR is guided to $2.058
  billion to $2.063 billion.

- **Going concern:** No going concern issues. The business is highly liquid, cash
  generative, and now GAAP profitable at the operating line.

- **Material risks identified:** The FY2026 Form 10-K risk factors and the earnings
  release forward-looking statements cite, among others: ability to sustain
  profitability, customer retention, reliance on third-party cloud infrastructure and
  large language models, competition including new potentially disruptive
  technologies, macroeconomic and geopolitical effects, and the price volatility of
  the Class A common stock. The 10-Q also flags an ongoing India corporate income tax
  appeal ($1.9 million assessment) and a Romania tax audit appeal, and a $27.1 million
  reserve for gross unrecognised tax benefits.

- **Governance observations:** Two facts sit alongside each other and should be read
  together. The board has an active $500 million repurchase authorisation dated 5
  March 2026 and the company has been buying stock steadily, including at $9.63 in
  early May. The founder-CEO has a 10b5-1 plan running to 14 October 2026 to sell up
  to 2.975 million shares. Readers may draw their own inference; this report draws
  none.

## Overall Summary

**No recommendation is issued.** Version 1.0 carried a flat "BUY" with the phrase
"highly asymmetric risk/reward entry point". That has been withdrawn. This document
is a factual analysis, not advice, and neither Mick Pavey nor DIY Investors is
authorised to give investment advice.

**The case that the fundamentals have inflected.** UiPath crossed into GAAP operating
profitability in Q1 FY2027 for the first time in its history, with a $27.987 million
operating profit against a $16.412 million loss a year earlier. Revenue grew 17% to
$418.382 million, gross margins held at 82% GAAP, non-GAAP operating margin expanded
from 20% to 22%, and adjusted free cash flow reached $130.268 million. The balance
sheet carries zero debt and $1.4157 billion of cash and marketable securities, about
$2.68 per diluted share. Management raised full-year revenue guidance to $1.776 to
$1.781 billion, above the $1.765 billion analyst consensus, and buybacks have cut the
basic share count from 548.451 million to 523.584 million year-on-year.

**The case against, which v1.0 did not put.** ARR growth of 12% is running below
revenue growth of 17%, which suggests the revenue line is flattered by timing rather
than by underlying subscription expansion; ARR is the metric management itself calls
its key performance indicator. Net new ARR was only $49 million in the quarter.
Dollar-based net retention of 109% is positive but modest for a company on this
multiple. The GAAP operating profit of $27.987 million is struck after adding back
$53.310 million of stock-based compensation to reach the $92.491 million non-GAAP
figure, so the majority of "profitability" on the non-GAAP measure is share-based pay
excluded. The effective tax rate was 45.0%. The company paid $149.403 million for
acquisitions in the quarter, adding $60.4 million of goodwill and $80.1 million of
intangibles, which is a different growth story from pure organic expansion. The
founder-CEO is a seller through 14 October 2026. And the company's own repurchase
price fell from an average $11.47 in the quarter to $9.63 in early May.

**What would settle it.** Q2 FY2027 results, due around late August 2026, against the
$395 to $400 million revenue guide and the $1.929 to $1.934 billion ARR guide. Net
new ARR is the number to watch, not revenue.
