## Introduction

Americas Gold and Silver Corp (NYSE American: USAS; TSX: USA) is a North American precious metals producer positioning itself as a leading primary silver miner. Its core assets are the Galena Complex in the Coeur d'Alene silver district of Idaho (now including the recently acquired adjacent Crescent mine), the Cosala Operations in Sinaloa, Mexico (where the high-grade EC120 orebody declared commercial production on 1 January 2026), and a 51/49 antimony joint venture with United States Antimony that targets critical-mineral domestic supply [8, 13, 14]. The company reports in US dollars. The shares closed the latest ShareScope session at approximately $3.99, having collapsed roughly 60% from the 12-month intraday high near $10.30 set in March 2026, but still sitting well above the sub-$2.50 base of a year ago. This report covers the fully reported FY2025 results (year ended 31 December 2025), the transformational Q1 2026 turnaround, the Q2 2026 production update and 2026 guidance, the balance sheet and going-concern position, the consensus and my own forecasts for FY2026, and a technical read of the 12-month chart.

## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis

- **Turnover:** Revenue has climbed steadily from $95.2m (FY2023) to $100.2m (FY2024) to $117.9m (FY2025), then inflected violently higher in Q1 2026 to $67.8m in a single quarter - a 187% year-on-year increase versus $23.5m in Q1 2025 [income CSV; 6, 8]. The step-change is driven by the EC120 commercial-production start, a 76% rise in silver production and a 148% jump in the realised silver price to an average of roughly $79.48/oz in Q1 2026 [8]. ShareScope consensus has FY2026 turnover at $356.4m (+202%), rising to $605.7m in FY2027 and $690.0m in FY2028 [forecasts CSV].
- **Gross profit and margin:** FY2025 gross profit turned positive at $11.8m (10.0% margin) after two years of gross losses (-$6.3m FY2023, -$6.6m FY2024) [income CSV; ratios CSV]. Q1 2026 gross profit was $37.1m, a ~55% gross margin, reflecting the operating leverage of high silver prices over a largely fixed cost base.
- **Operating profit:** FY2025 booked a standardised operating loss of -$25.9m, but Q1 2026 swung to a +$27.1m operating profit [income CSV]. The EBITDA margin moved from -4.3% (FY2025) to a forecast 49.1% (FY2026), 58.3% (FY2027) and 64.8% (FY2028) on ShareScope numbers [ratios CSV].
- **Pre-tax and net result (watch the one-offs):** FY2025 recorded a pre-tax loss of -$82.8m and a net loss of -$87.4m, materially worse than the -$41.5m and -$38.2m of FY2024 [income CSV; P&L summary 1-5]. The deepening FY2025 loss was driven largely by non-cash fair-value adjustments on metals-contract (delivery) liabilities as forward gold and silver prices rose, plus impairment-style other charges of ~-$51.6m in the "other income/expense" line [income CSV]. Q1 2026 returned to a +$16.7m pre-tax and +$10.0m net profit even after absorbing a further $12.5m non-cash loss on those metals-contract liabilities, partly offset by a $3.0m derivative gain on gold-price protection contracts [6, 7]. The Q2 2026 settlements of the Sprott silver and IRC gold delivery obligations are important because they remove the source of these recurring fair-value losses going forward [9-12].
- **EPS:** Reported EPS moved from -$0.40 (FY2023) and -$0.43 (FY2024) to -$0.33 (FY2025), then to a positive +$0.03 in Q1 2026 [P&L summary; income CSV]. Consensus EPS is $0.291 (FY2026), $0.611 (FY2027) and $0.709 (FY2028) [9, forecasts CSV].

### Balance Sheet Analysis

- **Net cash / net debt:** The balance sheet was transformed by a $132.3m bought-deal placement at $4.00/share in December 2025, which also funded the $20m cash portion of the Crescent acquisition [19, 20]. As at 31 March 2026 the company held ~$122.4m cash against total borrowings of ~$60.2m (a secured Term Loan Facility of ~$53.2m plus a ~$5.8m credit facility), i.e. a net cash position of roughly $62m (ShareScope net borrowing -$74.1m) [balance-sheet CSV; 18]. Cash was ~$89m at 30 June 2026, down from Q1 as planned growth capital was deployed - still net cash [6].
- **Share count:** Shares in issue rose from ~273.5m (end 2025) to ~320.4m at 31 March 2026 after the December placement, ~326.9m on the latest TTM basis, and to roughly 337m following the ~7.96m (Sprott) and ~2.65m (IRC) shares issued in Q2 2026 [balance-sheet CSV; 16, 17]. Average diluted shares for Q1 2026 were ~345.8m. This is a heavily dilutive growth story - shareholders have funded the turnaround.
- **Book value:** Shareholders' funds (NAV) were ~$221.6m at 31 March 2026, or roughly $0.69-0.73 per share [balance-sheet CSV; ratios CSV]. At ~$3.99 the shares trade at ~5.7x book (Price to NAV), so this is emphatically not an asset-value play - the valuation rests entirely on the forecast earnings ramp.
- **Pension:** There is an under-funded defined-benefit pension liability at the Galena Complex of a few million dollars requiring ~$1.1m of annual funding - immaterial to the thesis but noted [28; balance-sheet CSV].

### Cash Flow Analysis

- **Operating cash flow:** FY2025 operating cash flow was negative at approximately -$12.5m; the TTM figure to Q1 2026 had recovered to +$21.9m [cash-flow CSV]. Q1 2026 itself was still slightly negative (-$3.9m) on working-capital build as production scaled.
- **Capex and free cash flow:** Capital expenditure was $69.2m in FY2025 and is guided sharply higher to ~$157.3m in FY2026 as the Galena No.3 shaft modernisation, Crescent development and EC120 ramp are funded, before falling back to ~$83.7m (FY2027) and ~$56.9m (FY2028) [forecasts CSV]. Consequently free cash flow was -$73.2m in FY2025 and is forecast at -$21.3m in FY2026, turning strongly positive at +$212.0m (FY2027) and +$290.3m (FY2028) on consensus [forecasts CSV].
- **Cash balance:** Closing cash was ~$39m at end-2025, ~$122.4m at 31 March 2026 (post placement), and ~$89m at 30 June 2026 [cash-flow CSV; 6, 15].

### Management Commentary and Audit Notes

- **Post-period trading (Q2 2026):** Consolidated Q2 2026 silver production was 664,971 oz (624,343 oz sold), split Cosala 337,270 oz and Galena 327,701 oz - down 15% on Q1 on the Galena 4900-Level electrical fire [1-4]. The No.3 Shaft Phase 2 modernisation completed in June 2026, lifting hoisting capacity ~150% (to ~105 short tons/hour) and skip payload 40% - management frames this as a transformational de-risking event for Galena and Crescent [5-7]. Full-year 2026 guidance of 3.2-3.6m oz silver at AISC $30-35/oz was reaffirmed, H2-weighted [11].
- **Strategy:** Management (CEO Paul Andre Huet) is positioning USAS as a leading North American primary silver producer levered to structural global silver deficits and rising industrial/AI-infrastructure demand, with an added antimony critical-minerals angle via the United States Antimony JV [12, 13, 14]. The Q2 settlement of legacy metal-delivery obligations (>$85m of variable, debt-like obligations eliminated) simplifies the balance sheet and increases leverage to record silver prices [11].
- **Going concern (material):** Despite the strong cash balance, Q1 2026 filings carried going-concern disclosures. Management flagged material uncertainties over sustaining cash-flow-positive operations, covenant compliance (a waiver was recently required on a SAF Group facility) and execution of the capital-optimisation plan [21, 22]. This is the single most important caveat in the file and should temper position sizing.
- **Key risks:** Execution risk on the Galena No.3 shaft optimisation and Crescent development [23]; mining-hazard interruptions (the Galena fire, a regional wildfire near Crescent) [4]; ongoing security risk from organised crime around Cosala in Sinaloa, which has previously disrupted supply chains and mill throughput [24-26]; and high sensitivity to silver/copper/lead prices and inflation [23, 27]. Financial-health screens are middling-to-weak (Altman Z 2.4, Piotroski F around 4) though improving off a low base [ratios CSV].

This is a genuine special-situation / turnaround: a formerly loss-making, chronically dilutive junior silver producer that has inflected to profitability (Q1 2026 net profit $10.0m, EPS +$0.03) on the EC120 start-up and a spectacular silver price, and that has just recapitalised (net cash ~$62m at Q1, ~$89m cash at end-June) and cleaned up its legacy metal-delivery obligations. If the H2-weighted 2026 guidance and the FY2027 consensus ($605.7m revenue, $0.61 EPS, +$212m FCF) are delivered, the forward multiples (P/E 6.9x and EV/EBITDA 3.3x for 2027) are cheap for that growth. Against that stand real risks: an explicit going-concern disclosure, a covenant waiver, heavy dilution, a high AISC of $30-35/oz, Mexican security exposure, and total dependence on a silver price that is currently extraordinary and could mean-revert.

On the fundamentals alone the risk/reward past $3.50 is attractive for a small, high-conviction speculative position. But the chart is still in a confirmed downtrend and has not yet based, so chasing here risks buying before the retrace completes. Balancing the two, my rating is **HOLD / SPECULATIVE BUY on a hold of $3.50** - accumulate into the $3.50-$4.00 support zone with a stop below $3.40, add on a reclaim of $4.85, and size small given the going-concern flag. This is explicitly a special-situation, silver-price-levered call, not a core holding.
