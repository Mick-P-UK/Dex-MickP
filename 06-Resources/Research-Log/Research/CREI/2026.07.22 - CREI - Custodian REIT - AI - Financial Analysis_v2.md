## Introduction

Custodian Property Income REIT (CREI) is a UK diversified real estate investment trust that owns a portfolio of 174 commercial properties valued at £669.3m as at 31 March 2026, weighted towards industrial (42%), retail warehouse (22%) and offices (16%) [1]. The trust targets smaller, regional lot-sized assets across the UK, offering an enhanced yield versus larger-lot institutional-scale properties. The shares closed the latest ShareScope session at approximately 90p, sitting just below the 12-month high of 92p and trading at a modest ~9% discount to the 99.7p FY2026 EPRA NTA per share. This report covers the FY2026 results (year ended 31 March 2026), current portfolio positioning, dividend cover, catalysts, risks and consensus forecasts for FY2027, plus a technical read of the 12-month price chart.

## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis
- **Rental income:** Like-for-like contractual rent grew to £49.2m in FY2026 from £43.9m in FY2025, reflecting active rent reviews and the drop-through of newly acquired portfolios [1]. Reported net rental income for FY2025 was £39.5m [2].
- **EPRA earnings and EPS:** EPRA EPS grew 3.3% to 6.3p (FY2025: 6.1p), with absolute EPRA earnings of ~£26.8m in FY2025. Growth was driven by rent reviews, new lettings, and accretive capex on refurbished units [1, 2].
- **Operating profit:** Total income of £61.6m less operating expenses of £13.3m produced strong operating leverage [3].
- **PBT and net profit:** IFRS PBT jumped to £48.3m in FY2026 (FY2025: £38.2m) - the delta over EPRA earnings reflects the positive revaluation movement across the portfolio (like-for-like +2.7%) and gains on disposal at a 23% premium to pre-offer valuations [1, 3]. As a UK REIT, tax on qualifying property rental income is nil, so post-tax profit equals PBT at £48.3m.
- **Basic EPS:** 10.4p in FY2026 (FY2025: 8.7p), boosted by the revaluation swing [1, 3]. Note the material gap versus the recurring 6.3p EPRA figure - revaluations are volatile, EPRA is the more useful earnings anchor for dividend cover.

### Balance Sheet Analysis
- **Portfolio value:** £669.3m at 31 March 2026 vs £622.8m at 31 March 2025, growth from acquisitions plus a like-for-like uplift of 2.7% [1, 2].
- **NAV / EPRA NTA per share:** 99.7p at FY2026 year-end (FY2025: 96.1p), up 3.7% [4]. Total shareholders' funds £486.7m (FY2025: £423.5m) [2].
- **Debt:** Total debt £183.8m (FY2025: £172.3m); net debt £173.8m (FY2025: £166.0m) [2, 4].
- **Loan-to-value:** Net gearing/LTV improved to 25.9% (FY2025: 27.9%) despite the absolute-debt increase, because the equity/valuation base grew faster than borrowings [1]. This is a conservative LTV for a UK diversified REIT and gives cushion against modest valuation shocks.
- **Cost of debt and hedging:** Weighted average cost of debt rose to 4.1% (FY2025: 3.9%) after the August 2025 £20m loan repayment and RCF resizing. 65% of borrowing is fixed - a reasonable hedge though not fully insulating [1].
- **Debt maturity actions:** The £20m loan expiring in August 2025 was repaid, and the RCF headline was lifted from £50m to £75m, giving fresh dry powder for capex and tuck-ins [1].
- **Share count:** Grew during FY2026 via corporate-acquisition share issuance (Grove Court ~24.1m new shares; Scorpion ~4.9m new shares). This dilution has been NAV-neutral - shares issued at adjusted NAV, so per-share metrics are unimpaired [1, 2].

### Cash Flow Analysis
- **Operating cash flow:** £27.9m in FY2026 (FY2025: £25.0m) - solid double-digit growth driven by higher passing rents and a bigger portfolio [1].
- **Capex:** £9.5m invested in refurbishment programmes (Plymouth, Biggleswade, Kettering industrials plus a Carlisle drive-through) - accretive to income [2].
- **Property acquisitions:** Aggregate £63.8m of gross portfolio value acquired, but structured as majority-share corporate deals so the cash outflow on the cash flow statement was only £15.3m (FY2025: nil) [1, 3]. This is the linchpin of the strategy - scaling without cash equity issuance during a NAV-discount period.
- **Disposals:** £19.9m of proceeds at a 23% premium to pre-offer book value - recycling capital out of weaker assets [2].
- **Dividends paid:** £27.4m cash out (FY2025: £27.2m) [1].
- **Financing:** Net financing outflow of £22.6m (FY2025: £31.3m outflow) - includes the £20m loan repayment offset by RCF draws [1].
- **Cash balances:** Opening £10.1m, closing £12.3m per cash flow statement [4].

### Management Commentary and Audit Notes
- **Tone:** Confident, operationally focused. Management pointed to a "positive underlying sentiment towards listed real estate" while flagging elevated long-term interest rates and geopolitical tensions (Middle East) as continuing hurdles [2, 3, 9].
- **Strategic differentiation:** The share-for-share corporate acquisition strategy targeting family-owned property portfolios is the standout narrative. It solves succession problems for vendors on a tax-efficient basis while giving CREI accretive assets without diluting per-share NAV [1, 4, 5].
- **Latent income:** Management repeatedly emphasises the ~13-14% embedded latent rental growth in the standing portfolio (43% for the newly-added Scorpion assets), a key value-creation lever independent of macro cycles [3, 8].
- **Board/audit notes:** No adverse audit comments, going-concern flags or emphasis-of-matter items surfaced in the sources. The Scorpion board resigned on completion of the acquisition (expected in a corporate takeover). CREI's own board and management remain in place [11]. The next quarterly dividend declaration is scheduled for 23 July 2026 - a near-term catalyst [7].
- **Occupancy:** 92.4% at 31 March 2026 vs 91.1% a year earlier - a tangible improvement narrowing the vacancy tail [8].

## Overall Summary and Recommendation

Custodian REIT combines a well-covered 6.6% dividend yield (6.0p on 90p) with a distinctive, tax-efficient scaling strategy that adds assets without cash equity dilution during a discount-to-NAV period. FY2026 delivered rental growth, occupancy improvement, tighter LTV, and 3.7% NAV growth despite a modestly higher cost of debt. Consensus FY2027 EPS of 6.3p keeps the dividend covered at ~105%, and the 13% latent rental reversion embedded in the portfolio underwrites forward income growth independent of macro tailwinds.

Trading at a ~9% discount to the 99.7p NAV, CREI offers a defensible income entry point with modest capital-return upside as the discount narrows. The technical set-up is aligned with the fundamentals - a bullish MA stack, base breakout in progress, and RSI/MACD with headroom. The main watch-outs are interest-rate sensitivity (part-hedged), the 16% office weighting where management is actively reducing exposure, and macro risk to valuation trajectories.

**Recommendation: BUY** as a covered-income core REIT holding, with the caveat that this is more of a "boring is beautiful" total-return call than a special-situation snap-back. Key technical stop: **84p** (loss of first support); harder stop 76p (base low). Upside pivot: a daily close above **92p** targets NAV parity at 100p, then the 105-108p range. Dividend visibility, share-for-share acquisition optionality, and a 6.6% yield make this a compound-and-hold candidate rather than a trade.
