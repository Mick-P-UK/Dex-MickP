## Introduction

Custodian Property Income REIT (CREI) is a UK-listed, internally externally-managed real estate investment trust that owns a diversified portfolio of over 170 smaller lot-sized regional commercial properties (industrial, retail warehouse, other retail and office) let to more than 400 tenants. The strategy is built around an enhanced income yield from smaller, more granular assets in the regions rather than trophy assets in London, funded conservatively and paid out as a fully covered quarterly dividend. Shares are trading around 89.4p at the time of writing (12-month chart, 22 July 2026), giving the company a market capitalisation of approximately GBP425-440m. This report covers the FY26 full-year results announced on 10 June 2026, consensus forecasts for FY27, recent news to 22 July 2026, and a full technical read of the 12-month price chart.

## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis

- **Turnover / rental income** grew again in FY26, with total revenue of GBP52.4m (FY25: GBP48.0m) and rental revenue of GBP45.4m (FY25: GBP42.8m), driven by three portfolio acquisitions (Grove Court, Merlin and Scorpion, combined GBP63.8m) and successful lettings / rent reviews [1]. Annualised net rental income at the year end stood at GBP41.6m, up from GBP39.0m in FY25.
- **Gross profit (net rental income) margin** remained very high at c.92% of annualised passing rent (FY25: c.95%) - the modest step down reflects a fuller absorption of newly acquired assets and property costs on the larger portfolio.
- **Operating profit before valuation gains** was GBP35.8m in FY26 (FY25: GBP33.9m), a 68% margin against total revenue. Statutory IFRS operating profit was GBP55.5m (FY25: GBP45.5m), boosted by property revaluation uplifts.
- **PBT (IFRS)** jumped to GBP48.3m in FY26 (FY25: GBP38.2m; FY24: GBP14.9m), largely reflecting a swing back to positive fair-value movements on the portfolio as the UK regional property market steadied. Under EPRA, earnings were GBP29.0m (FY25: GBP26.8m).
- **EPS**: EPRA EPS rose to 6.3p (FY25: 6.1p; FY24: 5.8p), a genuine grind up in recurring earnings. IFRS EPS was 10.4p (FY25: 8.7p; FY24: 3.4p), inflated by fair-value movements. Anchor on the EPRA figure for cash-generative capacity - it is the number that actually pays the dividend.
- **Dividend cover** at 104.8% on EPRA earnings comfortably supports the 6.0p distribution and is a material improvement on 101.3% in FY25 [2].

### Balance Sheet Analysis

- **Portfolio value**: GBP669.3m across 174 properties at 31 March 2026 (FY25: GBP594.4m across a smaller portfolio) - the GBP74.9m uplift reflects GBP63.8m of acquisitions, GBP19.9m of disposals (at an aggregate 23% premium to pre-offer valuations) and net revaluation gains [1].
- **NAV / EPRA NTA per share**: 99.7p at 31 March 2026, up from 96.1p at FY25 - the third consecutive uplift and consistent with the sector's tentative recovery in capital values.
- **Gross assets**: GBP614m at FY25; FY26 gross assets are higher given the enlarged portfolio (not disclosed in the notebook to the pound).
- **Debt**: gross borrowings GBP185m (FY25: GBP175m); net debt / net borrowings GBP175m; LTV 25.9% (FY25: 27.9%). The GBP20m fixed-rate loan that matured in FY26 was refinanced using the extended and enlarged GBP75m RCF (previously GBP50m) - which pushed the weighted average cost of debt from 3.9% to 4.1%, with 65% of drawn debt still fixed. Weighted average debt maturity at end-FY25 was 4.5 years; FY26 disclosure not spelled out in the notebook.
- **Share count**: 494.06m in issue at 31 March 2026 (FY25: 440.85m), with 6.05m held in treasury following the buyback (avg 79.1p, 17.7% discount to dividend-adjusted NAV). Weighted average diluted count was 461.1m.
- **Portfolio metrics**: EPRA occupancy 92.4% (FY25: 91.1%), ERV GBP55.6m vs contracted rent GBP49.2m (13% latent reversion), WAULT to break 5.1 years.

Operating cash conversion looks intact; the risk to watch is that a growing RCF balance nudges finance costs higher over time.

### Management Commentary and Audit Notes

- **Post-period trading updates**: no Q1 FY27 trading update has yet been issued. The next scheduled dividend declaration is 23 July 2026 (tomorrow, in this report's frame) [22].
- **Strategy**: Managing Director Richard Shepherd-Cross reiterated the smaller lot-sized regional strategy and highlighted a differentiated growth lever - majority-share corporate acquisitions of privately owned property companies, using CREI's REIT tax status as a tax-efficient succession solution for family offices. That is a genuinely unusual capital allocation tool and is producing NAV-accretive share issuance while conventional equity raises remain unattractive at a NAV discount [1,4].
- **Outlook**: Chairman David MacLellan flagged that if long-term interest rates and inflation stay elevated, income and income growth will drive most of total returns in this cycle - a defensive framing that fits CREI's own portfolio. Management expects the property recovery to continue and to be led by rental growth rather than yield shifts.
- **Capital allocation**: 6.0p FY27 dividend target reiterated; buyback continues (further GBP2m approved May 2026); RCF extended to November 2028 and upsized to GBP75m.
- **Going concern**: unqualified. Directors adopted the going concern basis after a stress-tested 12-month and 3-year assessment with headroom to covenants and REIT tests.
- **Audit**: FY26 external audit report was entirely unqualified with no emphasis-of-matter items.

## Overall Summary and Recommendation

CREI is a well-run, conservatively financed regional REIT that has just posted a strong FY26: EPRA EPS 6.3p, NAV 99.7p, dividend 6.0p fully covered at 105%, LTV a comfortable 25.9%, and a genuinely differentiated growth engine (share-for-share corporate acquisitions). The shares trade at c.89-90p, a c.10% discount to NAV, on a c.6.7% fully covered yield. The technical picture is constructive - full bullish MA alignment, rising OBV, positive MACD - but ADX and A/D flag that the rally lacks strong institutional momentum, and price is bumping into the year's double-top resistance at 91-92p.

This is a **HOLD with a positive bias** for income-focused portfolios, tipping to **BUY on a decisive break above 92p** or on a pullback to the 87p shelf. The story here is not capital gain fireworks - it is a covered mid-single-digit yield plus a slow NAV grind and a plausible re-rating catalyst if the sector's NAV discount closes as interest-rate expectations soften. The lack of a Q1 FY27 update and the modest A/D divergence are reasons not to chase the current price.

Key technical stop: sustained close below 83p (breaks the year's horizontal support and the 200-day SMA).
Key upside pivot: daily close above 92p on above-average volume, targeting 96p then 99p (NAV).
