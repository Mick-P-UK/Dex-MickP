## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis
- **Turnover trends and growth drivers:** Customer growth remains exceptionally
strong, with the total customer base increasing by 23.3% to 1.43 million by the
end of FY26 [5]. This was driven by 10.3% organic growth and the acquisition of
193k broadband customers from TalkTalk [5]. Despite this volume growth, overall
organic services growth was slower (7.6%) due to intense competition in the 
Energy and Broadband markets [5]. Analyst consensus anticipates total turnover 
to reach £1,922.0m for FY26 [4].
- **Gross profit and margin analysis:** In H1 FY26, gross profit stood at 
£157.6m, which was a 6.1% decrease from the £167.8m reported in H1 FY25 [6]. 
Management noted this was largely due to a revised phasing of certain energy 
industry costs, such as metering, which heavily impacted the first half [7].
- **Operating profit performance:** Operating margins face headwinds from 
regulatory and macroeconomic shifts. Specifically, the Ofgem review of 
operating cost allowances within the energy price cap, alongside increases to 
National Insurance and the National Living Wage, have elevated administrative 
costs [8]. 
- **PBT and net profit commentary:** Adjusted PBT for H1 FY26 dropped 29.5% to 
£32.5m, down from £46.1m in H1 FY25 [7]. In their April 2026 update, management
guided that full-year adjusted PBT will land at the bottom end of their 
£132m-£138m target range [9]. This softer outcome was heavily influenced by 
reduced energy consumption during an unseasonably warm winter [9].
- **EPS implications:** Adjusted EPS for H1 FY26 came in at 31.5p [10]. For the
full year, the consensus ShareScope estimate projects EPS to hit 124.5p [4].
