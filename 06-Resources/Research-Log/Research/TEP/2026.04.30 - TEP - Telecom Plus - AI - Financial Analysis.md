## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis
- **Turnover trends and growth drivers:** The business continues to experience 
exceptional customer growth. In H1 FY26, revenues rose to £744.5m (up from 
£697.8m in H1 FY25), driven by a 19% increase in customer numbers to 1.38 
million [4, 5]. By the end of FY26, total customer growth hit 23.3% (including 
broadband customers acquired from TalkTalk) with organic customer growth at a 
robust 10.3% [6, 7].
- **Gross profit and margin analysis:** H1 FY26 gross profit stood at £157.6m 
[4]. While customer numbers soared, energy pricing competition has been fierce.
Rivals have offered fixed tariffs well below the Ofgem price cap, squeezing 
margins and causing a slight uptick in churn (14.2% in FY26 vs 13.7% in FY25) 
[8, 9].
- **Operating profit performance:** Administrative costs have been impacted by 
inflationary pressures, specifically the increases in National Insurance and 
the National Living Wage, as well as reduced operating cost allowances within 
the Ofgem energy price cap [10, 11].
- **PBT and net profit commentary:** Adjusted pre-tax profit for H1 FY26 was 
£32.5m, a drop from £46.1m in H1 FY25 [4]. Management notes this was primarily 
a "re-phasing" of energy costs [12, 13]. The April 2026 trading update confirms
that full-year adjusted PBT is expected to land at the bottom end of the 
£132m-£138m guidance range, largely due to unseasonably warm winter weather 
suppressing energy usage [6, 14].
- **EPS implications:** Adjusted EPS for H1 FY26 was 31.5p, down from 43.6p in 
H1 FY25 [4]. The full-year consensus of 125.0p represents a moderate 
anticipated growth curve into FY26 despite the tough H1 comparatives [15].

ongoing technology investments [16]. Leverage remains highly manageable at an 
anticipated 1.0x Net Debt/Adjusted EBITDA by the end of FY26 [14].

### Management Commentary and Audit Notes
- **Post-period trading updates:** The April 2026 trading update is highly 
relevant. While PBT will sit at the bottom of the target range due to weather 
factors, the TalkTalk integration is performing ahead of expectations, with 
14.5k customers already successfully upgraded and cross-sold [6, 23].
- **Capital Allocation Policy Update:** In a major shift announced in April 
2026, management revised its shareholder distribution policy. The company 
commits to returning at least 80% of adjusted post-tax profit to shareholders. 
Crucially, this will now be split: at least 50% of the payout will be ordinary 
dividends, and the remainder will be allocated to share buybacks or special 
dividends (depending on if shares are trading below fair value) [24, 25].
- **Going concern statements:** The half-year review conducted by KPMG

competition remain the primary headwinds [9, 28, 29].
