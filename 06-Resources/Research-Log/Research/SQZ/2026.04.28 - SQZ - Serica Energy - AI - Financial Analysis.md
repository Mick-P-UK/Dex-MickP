## Detailed Analysis

### P&L Analysis
- **Turnover trends and growth drivers:** In 2025, revenue fell 17% 
year-on-year to $601.4m (2024: $727.2m) [5, 6]. This decline was driven by 
unscheduled maintenance downtime at the Triton FPSO, which caused annual 
production to fall by approximately 20% to 27,600 boepd, alongside an 11% drop 
in realised oil prices to $67/bbl [7]. However, analysts forecast a massive 
top-line rebound in 2026 to GBP1,240.8m (+106.3%) as new acquisitions complete 
and production scales up [4].
- **Gross profit and margin analysis:** Gross profit for 2025 compressed 
severely to $64.7m (2024: $223.2m), reflecting the lower production volumes 
against fixed operating costs [5]. Cost of sales increased slightly to $536.7m 
(2024: $504.0m) [5]. 
- **Operating profit performance:** Operating profit before net finance costs 
and tax decreased to $112.0m in 2025, down from $186.5m in 2024 [5]. EBITDAX 
also saw a 45% decline to $210m (2024: $379m) [8, 9]. 
- **PBT and net profit commentary:** Pre-tax profit (PBT) halved to $80.3m in 
2025 (2024: $160.5m) [5]. Ultimately, the company swung to a post-tax loss of 
$51.8m for the year, primarily due to a massive taxation charge of $132.2m [5].
This included a $65m non-cash deferred tax hit recorded in Q1 2025 resulting 
from the UK Government's extension of the Energy Profits Levy (EPL) to 2030 [9,
10]. 
- **EPS implications:** Basic EPS for 2025 fell to a loss of 13 cents (2024: 24
cents profit) [5]. Forecasts for 2026 show a strong recovery with expected EPS 
of 58.1p [4].
