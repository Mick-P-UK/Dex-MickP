# Meet Cedric - Building Poppy (Episode Notes)

**Episode title (working)**: Building Poppy - Your Personal Assistant Project
**Format**: Two-parter
**Series pivot**: from share research automation to second-brain / PA
**Audience**: DIY Investors members - both .com (investing) and .ai (AI for investing) cohorts

---

## Why this episode matters

The Meet Cedric series so far has been investing-focused: stock research pipelines, NotebookLM workflows, ShareScope automation. This episode broadens the narrative.

Building Poppy demonstrates that the PAIDA approach (a family of specialised AI assistants) extends beyond investing into the rest of life and business. For DIY Investors members who run businesses, manage households, or just want their inbox under control, this is directly applicable.

It also opens the door to future episodes on second-brain workflows, knowledge management, and AI-augmented productivity that aren't tied to share research.

---

## Two-part structure

### Part 1: Building Poppy - The Discovery

**Hook**: "I've got an inbox problem. Here's how I'm building an AI assistant to fix it."

**Beats**:

1. **The problem** - inbox of 4000+ emails, can't find what matters, YouTube self-emails getting buried, accountant emails missed, newsletter overload
2. **The PAIDA approach explained** - Cedric (heavy local lifting), Annie (calendar specialist), Poppy (new - inbox / diary / PA)
3. **Why a separate project, not just one bigger Cedric** - separation of concerns, mobile access, different connector mix, focused scope
4. **The discovery questions** - showing how Cedric asks structured questions to elicit requirements (this is itself a teaching moment - applicable to any AI-assisted project)
5. **Decisions on screen** - categories, YT handling, self-improvement loop, cadence, boundaries
6. **The CLAUDE.md gets drafted** - watch the document come together
7. **The Settings doc** - connectors, knowledge files, Notion architecture
8. **Cliffhanger**: "Tomorrow we set Poppy live and see what she actually does"

**Estimated length**: 25-30 mins

**Best moments to capture**:
- The screenshot moment (showing real YT self-emails, demonstrating the detection signature)
- The DBox voice-to-text correction (authentic, shows the iterative refinement)
- The scheduling reality check (Projects don't run on cron) - turning a limitation into a v1/v2 plan
- The architecture decision (PAIDA hub in Notion)

### Part 2: Poppy in Action

**Hook**: "We built Poppy yesterday. Today we let her loose on a real inbox."

**Beats**:

1. **First-run bootstrap** - watch Poppy walk through Notion setup, label creation, backfill confirmation
2. **Real digest** - first morning digest produced live, on screen
3. **YT detection demonstration** - send a YT self-email mid-recording, watch Poppy log it correctly
4. **Rule suggestion in action** - show how the self-improvement loop surfaces patterns
5. **Mobile experience** - Slack DM ping on the phone, opening Poppy on mobile
6. **What needs tuning after week one** - honest review of misfires
7. **The roadmap** - n8n automation for fully scheduled digests, Telegram option, future capabilities

**Estimated length**: 20-25 mins

**Best moments**:
- The "watch her work" moments - real digests, real categorisation
- The honest tuning conversation (not pretending it's perfect first time)
- Mobile interaction (broadens the appeal beyond desk-bound viewers)

## Key teaching points (across both parts)

These are the takeaways the audience should leave with:

1. **AI assistants benefit from focused scope** - Poppy doesn't try to be Cedric. Specialisation works.
2. **Discovery questions beat blank-page prompting** - structured elicitation produces better instructions than "write me a CLAUDE.md for an assistant"
3. **CLAUDE.md is the contract** - it codifies what the assistant will and won't do. Hard rules go in writing.
4. **Memory must be external and persistent** - Notion database vs in-context only
5. **Self-improvement loops are rule-bound** - Poppy proposes, Mick approves. No silent drift.
6. **Boundaries protect users** - never sends without confirm, Never Touch list, summaries not verbatim
7. **Limitations matter** - Projects don't run on cron, so plan for it (calendar reminders v1, n8n v2)
8. **Vault + Drive = master + working copy** - source-of-truth in vault, live copy where the assistant can read it
