# Meet Cedric - Could an Open-Source Assistant Help Build Our Shared Brain?

## The hook

We have spent months building the MCSB - the Mick and Cedric Shared Brain - as a
structured layer on top of an Obsidian vault, with me (Cedric) reaching it through
Claude. This month a different tool arrived that does natively some of what we have
been hand-building: Hermes Agent, an open-source assistant from Nous Research that
runs on your own machine, remembers between sessions, writes its own reusable skills,
and can run jobs while you sleep. The question for this episode is not "should we
switch" - it is "does Hermes give MCSB a shortcut, and what does it cost us to find
out?" We are investigating, not converting.

## The episode arc

1. What MCSB is trying to be: one shared knowledge layer, fast for desktop work and
   reachable by the web assistant, so nothing about a stock, a portfolio or a decision
   ever has to be re-explained.
2. What Hermes brings to the table: persistent memory, self-written skills, a built-in
   scheduler, and a single gateway across Telegram, WhatsApp, Slack, email and the
   terminal. It is open-source (MIT), so the data and skills stay on your machine.
3. The overlap: a lot of MCSB's target design - remember context, encode the process
   as skills, run the morning news sweep automatically - is close to what Hermes ships
   out of the box.
4. The honest counterweight: Hermes is infrastructure, not an app. Skill poisoning is
   a real, current risk (an instruction hidden in a web page can be saved and acted on
   later), one CVE is already disclosed, and you still pay for the model behind it. It
   is not a beginner tool yet.
5. The investigation plan: stand up a paired NotebookLM notebook (Hermes + Claude +
   Obsidian) as the reading room, gather the primary sources, and test one narrow MCSB
   job on Hermes in a sandbox before deciding anything.

## Why it matters to the audience

- The build-versus-adopt question is one every DIY investor faces with AI now: do you
  hand-assemble a system, or lean on a tool that already does most of it?
- "It remembers and it compounds" is the real prize - an assistant that gets more
  useful the longer you use it - and it is worth understanding before you commit.
- Owning your stack (open-source, local data) has genuine appeal and genuine risk.
  The episode models weighing both rather than being sold one side.
- We investigate first, in a sandbox, on low-stakes data - never point a self-directed
  agent at broker logins to find out whether it is safe.

## Open questions to resolve on camera (or before)

- Does Hermes replace part of the MCSB build, sit alongside it, or just inform the
  design we already have?
- Where would the Obsidian vault fit - as Hermes's memory/skills store, or kept
  separate with a bridge?
- What is the smallest real MCSB task we could give Hermes as a fair test (e.g. the
  LSE regulatory-news morning sweep)?
