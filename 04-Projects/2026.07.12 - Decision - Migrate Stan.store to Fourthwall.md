# 2026.07.12 - Decision - Migrate Stan.store to Fourthwall

**Status:** Decided - move to Fourthwall (Free tier). Migration pending.
**Pillar:** DIY-Investors
**Created:** 2026-07-12
**Owner:** Mick

---

## Decision in one line

Move the DIY Investors store from Stan.store to Fourthwall, starting on the
Free tier, because Fourthwall removes the fixed monthly cost, supports
members-only video natively, and fits the planned PDF products. Migrate for
the cost and membership benefits, not for the YouTube shelf.

## Why this came up

- YouTube ad monetisation was switched on today (channel already in YPP,
  ~3,997 subscribers). That prompted a wider look at the store setup.
- Stan.store costs 29 USD per month and has sold no memberships since about
  February/March 2026, so it has become a fixed cost with no return.
- Members-only video is now a priority angle, plus several PDF ideas are
  planned to support the various aspects of DIY investing.

## The two options compared

| | Fourthwall | Stan.store |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly cost | Free tier 0 USD, or Pro ~15 USD/mo (annual, 180 USD/yr) / 19 USD monthly | From 29 USD/mo; Creator Pro 99 USD/mo |
| Platform fee | 5% on digital products AND memberships. Pro waives the 5% on digital products ONLY (memberships stay 5%) | 0% transaction fee (subscription only) |
| Payment processing | 2.9% + 0.30 USD | Standard Stripe (2.9% + 0.30 USD) |
| Members-only video | Native | Not native |
| Digital products (PDFs) | Yes; 5% Free, 0% on Pro | Yes |
| YouTube Merch Shelf | Native, co-developed with Google (geared to physical merch) | None |
| Best for | Merch, memberships, digital products, on-YouTube selling | Link-in-bio digital sellers and funnels |

## Financial rationale

- Stan charges 29 USD per month whether or not anything sells. Dead since
  ~Feb/Mar means roughly 116 to 145 USD already spent for no return, and
  348 USD per year if it continues.
- Fourthwall Free is 0 USD per month and only charges 5% when a membership or
  PDF actually sells. In a dead month it costs nothing. This directly removes
  the drag.
- Fourthwall Pro only makes sense once digital (PDF) sales are high enough
  that the waived 5% digital fee beats the ~15 USD/mo Pro cost. Breakeven is
  about 300 USD per month of digital sales (15 / 0.05). Below that, stay on
  Free. Pro does NOT change membership economics (memberships stay 5%).

## Fit for the plan

- Members-only video: native to Fourthwall and a good fit for an investing
  channel. Keep ~95% of membership revenue before payment processing.
- PDFs: the classic digital-product use case. Start on Free (5%), revisit Pro
  at the ~300 USD/mo threshold.

## Honest caveats

- The YouTube Merch Shelf (the "can be added to YPP" point) is designed to
  show physical products under videos. It does little for digital courses or
  memberships, so it should not drive the decision for this product mix.
- The saving is only "found money" if the memberships and PDFs actually sell.
  Changing tool removes the fixed cost; it does not create demand.
- Some steps (payout/bank setup, cancelling Stan) are Mick's to do directly.

## Recommendation

Switch to Fourthwall on the Free tier. Rebuild the storefront and links,
launch one or two PDFs and a members-only video tier, update all link-in-bio
references, then cancel Stan once Fourthwall is live. Reconsider Pro only when
PDF sales cross ~300 USD per month.

## Migration checklist

1. Create a Fourthwall account on the Free tier. Turn on 2-step verification.
2. Set up the shop basics: DIY Investors brand name, logo, colours. Custom
   domain can come later.
3. List everything currently live on Stan.store (links, any offers) so nothing
   is lost in the move.
4. Rebuild the link-in-bio homepage in Fourthwall to replace the Stan page.
5. Create the first one or two PDF products (start small, see ideas below).
6. Set up a Memberships tier with the members-only video perk. Decide price
   points (consider a single simple tier to start).
7. Connect payout / form of payment. NOTE: Mick to do this himself (banking
   details).
8. Optional: connect YouTube under Apps > YouTube if a product shelf is wanted
   later. Not a priority for a digital/membership mix.
9. Test one purchase end to end before announcing.
10. Update all links from Stan to Fourthwall: YouTube channel and video
    descriptions, social bios, newsletter, website.
11. Cancel the Stan.store subscription once Fourthwall is live and links are
    updated, to stop the 29 USD/mo. NOTE: Mick to do this himself.
12. Diarise a review once digital sales approach ~300 USD/mo to decide on Pro.

## PDF ideas to seed the launch

Short, practical guides that match the DIY Investors audience and recent work.
Each can double as the companion download for a members-only video.

1. The DIY Investor Starter Kit - choosing a platform and account type
   (ISA vs SIPP vs general account) and getting set up.
2. Building an Active 10 Portfolio - the concentrated 10-stock approach used
   for the UK and US Active 10 portfolios, and how to run it.
3. How to Read an RNS - decoding London Stock Exchange announcements and what
   actually moves a share price.
4. AI Tools for Investors - a practical guide to using ShareScope, NotebookLM
   and chat AIs for stock research (ties directly to the AI for Investors
   webinars).
5. The One-Page Stock Screening Checklist - the key ratios and filters to
   shortlist candidates worth a closer look.
6. Your Weekly Portfolio Review Routine - the risers/fallers and news-check
   habit, and when a move is worth acting on.

Suggested first launch: idea 1 (broad appeal, funnels new subscribers in) plus
idea 4 (leans on the AI angle the channel is known for) as the first
members-only video companion.
