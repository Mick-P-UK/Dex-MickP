**DIY Investors**

**12-Month Income Growth - Planning Session Notes**

*Prepared by Cedric (PAIDA) for Mick \| 4 July 2026*

1\. Purpose

A brainstorming and planning session to identify the best opportunities
to improve income from DIY Investors over the next 12 months.

Context: Mick is a fit 70-year-old with roughly 30 hours per week
available. This document captures the strategy agreed tonight plus a
running tally of income and automation ideas. Further ideas are to be
added in a follow-up session.

2\. The Core Reframe

At 70, with 30 hours a week, the aim is to convert expertise into
leveraged and recurring assets - not to work more hours.

The binding constraint is acquisition and pricing, not delivery
capacity. There is ample room to serve more members; what is missing is
a repeatable way to get in front of the right people, and a reason for
them to pay more. Every idea below is judged on the leverage ladder:
time-for-money, then product, then recurring, then owned asset.

3\. Membership Picture (the revenue base)

-   Inner Circle (.com): GBP 380/yr, approx 10 members.

-   Plaza Group (.com): Inner Circle plus a top-up premium. Annual
    figure TO BE CONFIRMED by Mick (changed this year).

-   Silver (.ai): GBP 360/yr, 6 members - 3 also hold .com/Plaza, 3 are
    .ai-only.

Key figures (verified by code):

-   Known recurring floor, excluding the Plaza premium: GBP 5,960/yr
    (Inner Circle 10 x 380 = 3,800; Silver 6 x 360 = 2,160).

-   Each new member is near-pure margin: +GBP 380 (Inner Circle) or +GBP
    360 (Silver).

-   Doubling headcount adds only about GBP 5,960/yr (plus Plaza) -
    modest. So pricing and tiering matter as much as member count.

-   Cross-sell headroom (near-zero acquisition cost): the 3 .ai-only
    members could each add Inner Circle (+GBP 1,140/yr); converting
    Inner Circle members to Silver adds more.

4\. Guiding Principles (the filters we agreed)

-   Time-savings and income are the same pipeline. The engine that
    reclaims Mick\'s mornings is also the deliverable stack for the
    premium tier - build once, benefit twice.

-   Any premium (Gold) tier must be one-to-many or self-serve. Never
    bespoke, per-member work - or it becomes a job, not an asset.

-   Price on value to the member (hours saved, ideas surfaced), not
    cost-plus.

-   \'Silver\' already implies \'Gold\'. The branding ladder exists; the
    top rung just needs building.

-   FCA financial-promotion caution throughout. Keep everything
    educational (how the tools and method work), never tips or \'buy
    this\'. Automated and live content raise this risk - keep a human
    review gate initially.

-   Integrate, don\'t rebuild. Much is already built as skills; freed
    hours must go to acquisition, not more tooling.

5\. Income Opportunities (round-one ranking)

-   Flagship \'AI for Investing\' evergreen product. Highest leverage,
    rides the AI wave, only Mick can teach it credibly. Build once, sell
    repeatedly.

-   Membership and pricing restructure. Raise Silver GBP 360 to GBP 380
    to match Inner Circle, and add a Gold tier built on the
    ShareScope/AI automation. Recurring compounds; a Gold tier also
    makes Silver look a bargain, helping conversion and retention.

-   Productise the automations as a premium benefit. Captures the
    ShareScope automation value as recurring income without a
    support-heavy standalone product (and keeps clear of FCA bother).

-   Affiliate / partnership income. ShareScope, SharePad, brokers.
    Near-zero marginal effort; likely being left on the table.

-   YouTube is treated as the growth engine feeding all four, not a
    direct income line.

6\. The Funnel That Emerged

-   Top: ShareScope pain-point content and YouTube videos (found via
    search/SEO).

-   Middle: a free \'Get More From ShareScope\' asset, plus a periodic
    AI+ShareScope livestream with advance registration - feeding
    newsletter nurture. The newsletter finally has a job: nurturing
    registrants.

-   Bottom: Silver / Inner Circle, converting up to Gold / Plaza.

7\. Automation and Content Ideas (running tally)

7.1 Weekday news to Notion to Mick\'s Diary (CROWN JEWEL)

Status: partly built - us-news-checker and lse-news-checker skills
exist. Gap: loop every portfolio, write qualifying items to Notion,
auto-draft the Diary post.

Verdict: daily time buy-back AND the core Plaza benefit. Scales
one-to-many. Do first. Run as a scheduled pipeline (n8n or Cowork
scheduled task), not tag-triggered. Keep a human review gate for FCA
safety at first.

7.2 Monthly 4-portfolio snapshots to annotate to post on .com

Status: mostly built - Poster Pete handles posting, sharescope-get-chart
captures images. Gap: auto-annotation (fill figures/commentary into a
template).

Verdict: monthly buy-back plus .com member content. A \'connect the
pieces\' job, low effort.

7.3 Portico Plaza weekend video (PP1 / PP2 real-money SIPP portfolios)

Status: manual. Gap: data pull and script draft.

Verdict: semi-automate only. Draft the script and pull data; Mick stays
on camera - the personal delivery is the product. Weekly buy-back plus
Plaza premium.

7.4 Webinar prep and NotebookLM repurposing engine

Status: infrastructure live - NotebookLM MCP link,
pdf-to-pptx-converter, ai4inv-webinar-processor. Gap: AX-tree NotebookLM
layers 2-5 (unbuilt); planned notebooks (Inner Circle, Plaza, AI for
Investing) not yet created.

Verdict: time buy-back on delivery AND a content-multiplication engine.
One webinar source sprays into deck, YouTube cut, newsletter items and a
member guide. Feeds acquisition. Strong keeper.

7.5 ShareScope pain-point mining (TOP-OF-FUNNEL KEYSTONE)

Status: idea. Uses AX-tree tooling to read ShareScope\'s in-app
discussion forum and theme the common problems.

Verdict: the first idea that attacks acquisition - the binding
constraint. Run in three separate stages: (a) reading the forum = market
research (legitimate); (b) a free \'Get More From ShareScope\' asset
(YouTube / mini-guide) = the funnel; (c) membership = the conversion.

Cautions: do NOT scrape-and-spam or post promo in their forum (terms
breach plus reputation/ban risk). Reframe as \'the man who teaches
ShareScope properly\' - this reduces ShareScope\'s own churn, opening a
partner / affiliate / co-marketing route where they might promote Mick.
Explore partnership before anything covert.

7.6 YouTube video preparation

Status: existing skills - content-extraction, title-generator,
key-takeaways, researcher-agent, image-cta-overlay, yt-weekly-stats-v2.

Verdict: not a new build - an OUTPUT of the content engine (7.4), aimed
at the ShareScope pain points. Reuse, don\'t rebuild.

7.7 YouTube livestream - \'How I use AI + ShareScope\'

Status: idea. Advertised in advance with registration.

Verdict: the conversion event that completes the funnel middle. Advance
registration captures emails for newsletter nurture. A live demo is
Mick\'s unfair advantage on display, self-selecting the exact buyer.

Cautions: run as a periodic signature event (e.g. quarterly), NOT weekly
(weekly = the Boot Camp treadmill trap). Repurpose each one heavily
(evergreen flagship video plus a month of clips). Highest FCA-risk
format because it is unscripted - use a loose script and guard-rails.
Judge by registrations and replay views, not live head-count. Decide
later: open stream (discovery/SEO) vs gated registration (list-building)
- possibly both.

8\. Technical Reality: Slack Automation

\@Claude in Slack (\'Claude Tag\') is reactive only. It responds when
tagged in a channel it has been invited to; it does not monitor channels
or fire on triggers. Basic tagging is on paid tiers (confirm exactly
what the USD 100 Max plan includes).

A daily automatic Mick\'s Diary post cannot run off tagging (that is
still manual triggering). It needs a scheduler: Claude Cowork scheduled
tasks (only while the computer is awake and the Desktop app is open) or,
more robustly, an automation layer such as n8n.

9\. Open Questions / To Confirm

-   Plaza Group annual price (Mick to confirm - changed this year).

-   Exact Slack features included in the USD 100 Max plan.

-   Gold tier price point (value-based, likely well above GBP 380 - pin
    once the automation output is visible).

-   Livestream: open vs gated (or both).

-   Whether to raise Silver to GBP 380 at next renewal.

10\. Suggested Next Steps (sequencing)

Because so much is already built, the theme is integrate, don\'t rebuild
- and freed hours go to acquisition.

1.  Wire the crown jewel: news to Notion to Mick\'s Diary as a scheduled
    pipeline (n8n). Biggest daily saving plus core Plaza value.

2.  Finish the monthly snapshot annotation step (mostly built).

3.  Stand up the webinar / NotebookLM repurposing engine so every
    webinar sprays out YouTube, newsletter and a guide.

4.  Run the ShareScope forum research pass; theme the top pain points.

5.  Produce the free \'Get More From ShareScope\' asset from those pain
    points.

6.  Plan the first quarterly AI+ShareScope livestream with a
    registration page.

7.  Design and price the Gold tier around the automation outputs; decide
    the Silver price.

8.  Explore a ShareScope partnership / affiliate conversation.
