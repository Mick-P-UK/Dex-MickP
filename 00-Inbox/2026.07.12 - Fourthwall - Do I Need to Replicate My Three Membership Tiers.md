# 2026.07.12 - Fourthwall - Do I Need to Replicate My Three Membership Tiers

## Context

On DIY-investors.com Mick currently runs three membership levels:

- Bronze - free
- Silver (a.k.a. Inner Circle) - paid
- Gold (a.k.a. Plaza) - higher paid

Question put to the Fourthwall NotebookLM: does this three-tier structure need
to be replicated inside Fourthwall as separate membership tiers, or should it
be restructured?

## Short answer

Yes - replicate all three tiers inside Fourthwall. The notebook explicitly
recommends mirroring the existing names and prices rather than restructuring.

## The five specific points

### 1. Does Fourthwall support multiple paid tiers plus a free tier?

Yes, natively. You can create as many tiers as you like, and one of them can
be priced at 0. Free and paid tiers display side by side on the Fourthwall
shop, which makes the upsell path visually obvious.

### 2. How are free members handled?

As lead capture / top of funnel. Bronze becomes an "email list you own"
inside the Fourthwall ecosystem - you get their details, you can message them
via Fourthwall's Messages feature, and you can steadily surface Silver /
Plaza upgrade offers to them.

### 3. Mirror or restructure?

Mirror. The notebook is unambiguous on this: if the three-tier structure is
already working, keeping familiar names and prices minimises confusion during
migration. The guidance is only to restructure if a tier is either not
resonating or not sustainable to deliver.

### 4. Gotchas when migrating existing paying members (the important one)

- No automated payment porting. Fourthwall is the Merchant of Record, so
  existing Silver and Inner Circle payers cannot be moved across silently.
  Each one has to manually re-subscribe on the Fourthwall page
  (yourshop.fourthwall.com/supporters/pricing). That is real churn risk.
- Mitigation: a dedicated switch-over email campaign, plus a Fourthwall
  promo code (e.g. first month free) to make re-entering card details feel
  worth their while.
- YouTube shelf gotcha: Google does not allow digital memberships on the
  YouTube Merch Shelf. If promotion runs via YouTube, links go in
  descriptions, pinned comments, and end screens - not the shelf.

### 5. Do tiers need distinct rewards?

No. Every perk lets you tick which tiers it applies to, so a perk can either
be shared across Silver + Plaza, or locked to Plaza only. Same flexibility
for Discord roles, private RSS feeds, posts, and members-only products.

## Recommendation

Set it up as:

- Bronze - 0
- Silver / Inner Circle - current Silver price
- Plaza / Gold - current Plaza price

Keep the names the audience already recognises. Plan the migration around a
comms campaign for existing paying members, because that manual re-subscribe
step is where drop-off will happen if it is rushed.

and Plaza churn during migration.

## Mick's addendum (2026.07.12)

The notebook's biggest flagged risk - mid-cycle churn from existing paying
members having to manually re-subscribe on Fourthwall - is not actually a
risk in Mick's situation, for one specific reason:

- All current DIY-investors.com paying members are on ANNUAL
  subscriptions, not monthly. Annual was chosen because it is cheaper than
  the monthly rate, and every current member has taken it.
- All current memberships expire at the end of February 2027.
- Mick handles renewal outreach 6 to 8 weeks before that, so the renewal
  window runs roughly early January 2027 to end of February 2027.

Because everyone is on the same annual cycle and nobody is mid-cycle, the
platform switch does NOT need a separate churn-mitigation campaign. The
Fourthwall migration should simply be lined up with the normal
January-February 2027 renewal window - the switch-over IS the renewal.
Renewal comms sell the move as part of "renew for the coming year, and
here is where you do it now".

Implication for the plan: the follow-on question the note originally
suggested (what the switch-over comms campaign should say to minimise
Silver / Plaza churn) is not needed as a separate piece of work. It folds
into the standard renewal comms that already run every January.
