# The Listed RPA and AI Automation Landscape

Beyond ServiceNow and UiPath: Four Alternative Plays on Enterprise Process Automation

Pegasystems [PEGA] | Appian [APPN] | SS&C Technologies [SSNC] | NICE Ltd [NICE]

Prepared by Cedric (PAIDA) for Mick Pavey | 8 July 2026

## 1. Executive Summary

This note surveys the listed companies, beyond ServiceNow [NOW] and UiPath [PATH], that help major enterprises implement robotic process automation (RPA) and AI-driven automation of routine corporate processes. Four names stand out as investable, focused exposures: Pegasystems, Appian, SS&C Technologies (owner of Blue Prism) and NICE Ltd.

The common pattern across all four is striking: demand indicators for AI and automation are strong (contract value, ARR and bookings growth), yet every one of these shares trades well below its 52-week high. The market is waiting for AI bookings to convert into recognised revenue and durable margins. For patient investors, this creates a potential opportunity set in what might be called the 'unloved automation layer' - the workflow and orchestration software that sits between raw AI models and real corporate processes.

- Fastest growth: Appian (+21% revenue, cloud +25%), now GAAP operating profitable for the first time.
- Best cash machine: SS&C, with a 39.5% adjusted EBITDA margin and raised full-year guidance.
- Biggest disconnect between bookings and revenue: Pegasystems, where cloud contract value grew 29% while reported revenue fell 9.6%.
- Cheapest on forward sales: NICE at roughly 1.8x, with AI ARR growing 66% and a debt-free balance sheet.

None of these observations constitutes a recommendation. This report is educational research to support your own decision-making.

## 2. Market Context: RPA Grows Up

Traditional RPA - software robots mimicking human clicks and keystrokes - has evolved into what analysts now call intelligent or agentic process automation. Everest Group has even rebranded its RPA vendor assessment as the Intelligent Process Automation Platform (IPAP) evaluation, reflecting the shift toward platforms that combine rules-based automation with generative and agentic AI to automate both repetitive and cognitive work.

The competitive field beyond NOW and PATH divides into two camps. First, focused platform vendors: Pegasystems, Appian, SS&C's Blue Prism, NICE and Verint. Second, big-cap platforms with major automation arms: Microsoft (Power Automate), Salesforce (MuleSoft and Agentforce), IBM (watsonx Orchestrate) and SAP (Signavio). Notably, UiPath's closest pure-play rival, Automation Anywhere, remains private, as do Celonis, Workato and Zoho - which concentrates the listed pure-play opportunity into the four names analysed here.

A key 2026 dynamic: AI agents are increasing interest in RPA rather than replacing it. Agents handle judgement and exceptions, but they still need a reliable execution layer to act across systems - which is exactly what these platforms provide.

## 4. Company Profiles

### 4.1 Pegasystems [PEGA] - The ACV Story

What it does: Enterprise workflow automation, decisioning and low-code development. Its Blueprint AI product helps enterprises redesign processes, with Pega's workflow engine providing governed execution. Clients are predominantly large banks, insurers, telcos and government bodies.

Q1 2026: Pega Cloud ACV grew 29% year on year (27% constant currency) and total ACV grew 12%. Free cash flow and operating cash flow both exceeded $200m. However, reported revenue fell 9.6% to $430m and GAAP net income dropped 62% to $32.8m, missing estimates - a consequence of large multi-year licence deals recognised in the prior-year quarter. Pega Cloud revenue itself rose 36% to $205m.

Guidance: FY26 revenue of about $2.0bn, free cash flow of $575m, ACV growth of 15%.

The investment debate: Bulls argue ACV is the true forward indicator and the revenue optics are an accounting artefact of the cloud transition; analysts forecast roughly 9% annual revenue growth over three years. Bears note the shares have been punished (market cap down roughly 42% in a year) and the Q1 miss was substantial. Recent news includes the Adopteq acquisition (January 2026) to migrate legacy Lotus Notes applications into Pega workflows, and a Leader position in Gartner's Magic Quadrant for Process Intelligence.

### 4.2 Appian [APPN] - The Reacceleration Story

What it does: Low-code process automation platform automating complex processes for large enterprises and governments for over 25 years. Its AI layer is deliberately AI-agnostic, letting clients swap underlying models without losing capability - a differentiated anti-lock-in pitch.

Q1 2026: Total revenue rose 21% to $202.2m; cloud subscriptions rose 25% to $124.5m - the fastest cloud growth in two years. Cloud net ARR expansion reached 115% (up from 112%), GAAP operating income turned positive at $3.2m, adjusted EBITDA was $26.6m, and the weighted Rule of 40 hit 42, its best reading since the metric was introduced. Nearly 40% of customers have purchased AI-inclusive licence tiers, and the DocCenter product processed more document pages in Q1 than in all of 2025 combined.

Guidance: FY26 revenue raised to $819-831m with adjusted EBITDA of $97-105m and non-GAAP EPS of $0.94-1.05. The buyback authorisation was doubled to $100m.

The investment debate: The smallest and historically least profitable of the four, but momentum is clearly building: eleven consecutive quarters of improving go-to-market efficiency, billings growth averaging 24% and seven-figure AI-driven wins in oil & gas and automotive. The shares nonetheless trade nearly 50% below their 52-week high, and customer acquisition costs remain elevated - the classic small-cap growth trade-off.

### 4.3 SS&C Technologies [SSNC] - The Compounder with a Blue Prism Kicker

What it does: A $6.7bn-revenue provider of investment, financial and healthcare software and services - and, via its 2022 acquisition of Blue Prism (the firm widely credited with coining the term RPA), a major force in enterprise-grade, governance-heavy automation favoured by banks and regulated industries.

Q1 2026: Record results: adjusted revenue up 8.8% to $1,648m (organic growth 5.0%), adjusted EBITDA up 10% to $651m at a 39.5% margin, and adjusted diluted EPS up 14.2% to $1.69. Operating cash flow rose 10% to about $300m, and $233m was returned to shareholders. Full-year guidance was raised to $6.66-6.82bn revenue.

The automation angle: SS&C launched Blue Prism WorkHQ in April 2026 - an agentic workflow orchestration platform coordinating automation, AI agents and human decision-making across enterprise workflows. Crucially, SS&C eats its own cooking: nearly 4,000 internal digital workers are deployed, which the CEO estimates may save a couple of hundred million dollars a year. Management targets a 40% EBITDA margin by Q4 2026.

The investment debate: The most defensive and cash-generative of the four, on roughly 21x earnings versus a software industry average near 39x. The trade-off: automation is a slice of a much larger fund-administration business, so this is diversified exposure rather than a pure play, and the balance sheet carries substantial debt ($7.65bn) from its acquisition history.

### 4.4 NICE Ltd [NICE] - The Cheap AI Transition

What it does: AI-native customer experience (CX) platform (CXone) with deep roots in attended automation - bots assisting human agents in contact centres - plus financial crime compliance via Actimize. The July 2025 Cognigy acquisition added conversational and agentic AI, now tightly integrated into CXone.

Q1 2026: Revenue rose 9.8% to $768.6m, beating guidance; cloud revenue grew 14.6% to $603.4m (12% excluding Cognigy). AI ARR grew 66% year on year, every CXone enterprise deal in the quarter included AI offerings, and international revenue grew about 30%. The company is debt-free, generated $148.8m of free cash flow, and repurchased a record $253m of shares. The cost: GAAP operating margin fell to 16.5% from 21.2% as the portfolio pivots to AI, and cloud net revenue retention of 107% shows some near-term pressure.

Guidance: FY26 revenue of $3.17-3.19bn (about 8% growth) with cloud growing 13-15%; full-year EPS guidance was raised to $10.98-11.18 (non-GAAP).

The investment debate: The cheapest of the four on forward sales (~1.8x) and arguably on earnings too, with a fortress balance sheet. Management is deliberately trading near-term pricing discounts for longer, larger AI automation commitments - a short-term margin drag for a long-term land-grab. The CEO also offers a note of realism: the volume of customer interactions actually managed by AI remains in single digits, meaning the runway is long but so is the wait.

## 5. Cross-Cutting Themes

Bookings lead, revenue lags. PEGA's ACV +29%, APPN's ARR expansion of 115% and NICE's AI ARR +66% all outpace reported revenue growth. Contract-value metrics are the leading indicators to watch each quarter.

Everyone is now 'agentic'. Blue Prism WorkHQ, Pega Blueprint, Appian's AI-agnostic layer and NICE Cognigy all pitch orchestration of AI agents plus humans plus bots. Product differentiation is narrowing; distribution and installed base matter more.

The market is sceptical. All four trade materially below their 52-week highs despite beats and raised guidance - a sector-wide de-rating rather than company-specific failure.

Private competition looms. Automation Anywhere and Celonis remain private; Microsoft's Power Automate is bundled into Microsoft 365 and is the most disruptive force on pricing at the low end.

Regulated industries are the moat. Banks, insurers, healthcare and government - where governance, auditability and security matter - are where these four win against cheaper tools.

## 6. Risks to Monitor

Conversion risk: AI pilots and bookings failing to convert into recognised revenue at the expected pace.

Bundling risk: Microsoft and Salesforce giving away 'good enough' automation inside existing enterprise agreements.

Disruption risk: agentic AI startups leapfrogging workflow platforms if enterprises decide they no longer need a heavy orchestration layer.

Company-specific: PEGA's licence-revenue lumpiness; APPN's small scale and elevated customer acquisition costs; SSNC's $7.65bn debt load; NICE's margin compression during its portfolio transition.

Macro: enterprise IT budget hesitancy amid tariffs, geopolitical tension and rate uncertainty, as flagged by SS&C management.
