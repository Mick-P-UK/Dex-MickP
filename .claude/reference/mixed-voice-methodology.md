# Mixed Voice Methodology for Research Papers

**Source Document:** 2026.02.16 - Strategic Dual Valuation Methodology (PGM research paper)
**Date Extracted:** 2026-02-16
**Analysis:** Comparison of v1 (AI-generated) vs v3 (Mick's personal edit)

---

## Overview

Mick's "mixed voice" for technical research papers balances **professional authority** with **personal humility** and **conversational warmth**. This methodology emerged from analyzing his edits to a 6,000+ word research paper on PGM mining valuation.

The mixed voice is characterized by **8 distinct patterns** that transform dry technical content into engaging, authentic analysis while maintaining credibility.

---

## The 8 Mixed Voice Patterns

### 1. Personal Attribution & Journey

**Pattern:** Reveals thought process and decision-making journey rather than presenting conclusions as received wisdom.

**Before (v1):**
> "Right, let's talk about valuing mining companies. Specifically, those in the Platinum Group Metals (PGM) sector."

**After (v3):**
> "Over the past few months, it's occurred to me that we need a different way to value mining companies and in this piece of original research, I've decided to focus on valuing mining companies in the Platinum Group Metals (PGM) sector."

**Key Phrases:**
- "it's occurred to me"
- "I've decided to focus on"
- "I've shied away from"
- "I've been thinking about"
- "In this research paper, I'm going to show you"

**Purpose:** Positions research as personal discovery/journey, not authoritative decree. Invites reader to follow thinking rather than accept conclusions blindly.

---

### 2. Humility Markers

**Pattern:** Injects personal perspective markers into assertions to avoid sounding omniscient.

**Before (v1):**
> "The evidence from the UK PGM sector demonstrates that relying on P/E ratios alone is insufficient, as it fails to capture the 'purchasing power' of investment capital..."

**After (v3):**
> "The evidence from the UK PGM sector demonstrates that relying on P/E ratios alone is insufficient, as it fails **(IMHO)** to capture the 'purchasing power' of investment capital..."

**Before (v1):**
> "The 'Ounces per £1' metric reveals a simple truth..."

**After (v3):**
> "**To me,** the 'Ounces per £1' metric reveals a simple truth..."

**Before (v1):**
> "The extrapolation approach must model the time-lag..."

**After (v3):**
> "**It seems obvious to me that** the extrapolation approach must model the time-lag..."

**Key Phrases:**
- "(IMHO)" embedded in technical analysis
- "To me,"
- "It seems obvious to me that"
- "I reckon"
- "As far as I'm concerned"

**Purpose:** Maintains authority while acknowledging these are informed opinions, not universal truths. Shows intellectual humility without undermining credibility.

---

### 3. Audience Specificity

**Pattern:** Uses precise audience identifiers aligned with ICP rather than generic terms.

**Before (v1):**
> "As the world shifts toward a hydrogen-based and carbon-conscious economy, those **investors** who have secured the maximum physical leverage..."

**After (v3):**
> "As the world shifts toward a hydrogen-based and carbon-conscious economy, those **diy-investors** who have secured the maximum physical leverage..."

**Key Changes:**
- "investors" → "diy-investors"
- Generic "readers" → Direct community address
- Implicit audience → Explicit community identity

**Purpose:** Creates in-group belonging and demonstrates content is tailored for specific audience with specific philosophy (DYOR, independent thinking).

---

### 4. Conversational Transitions

**Pattern:** Uses informal transitions in technical content to maintain warmth and personal ownership.

**Before (v1):**
> "**Right, let's** bring this all together."

**After (v3):**
> "**OK, let me** bring this all together."

**Before (v1):**
> "Track quarterly progress against DFS targets..."

**After (v3):**
> "**This means that we need to** track quarterly progress against DFS targets..."

**Key Changes:**
- "Right" → "OK"
- "let's" → "let me" (personal ownership)
- Implicit connections → Explicit bridges ("This means that we need to")
- "Now, I'll be honest" → "Now, to be honest with you"

**Purpose:** Prevents research papers from becoming dry or academic. Maintains conversational thread through dense technical material.

---

### 5. Clarity & Precision Additions

**Pattern:** Adds explicit scope clarifiers and hedging qualifiers to technical statements.

**Before (v1):**
> "Using **UK-listed** PGM miners—Valterra Platinum (VALT)..."

**After (v3):**
> "Using **example (UK-listed)** PGM miners—Valterra Platinum (VALT)..."

**Before (v1):**
> "Analysts apply a probability factor based on the stage of development..."

**After (v3):**
> "Analysts **typically** apply a probability factor based on the stage of development..."

**Before (v1):**
> "Tharisa's Karo project moved into the high-probability category..."

**After (v3):**
> "**As an example,** Tharisa's Karo project moved into the high-probability category..."

**Before (v1):**
> "To calculate the 'Future Ounce per £1', compare..."

**After (v3):**
> "To calculate the 'Future Ounce per £1 **(or $)**', compare..."

**Key Additions:**
- "example" to clarify illustrative nature
- "typically" to hedge generalizations
- "As an example," for transition clarity
- "(or $)" for currency flexibility
- "Of the three stocks analysed" for explicit scope

**Purpose:** Prevents overreach and acknowledges limitations of analysis. Shows intellectual rigor and attention to edge cases.

---

### 6. Softer Technical Language

**Pattern:** Chooses measured, less aggressive word choices in technical explanations.

**Before (v1):**
> "...precisely what this framework is designed to **pierce**."

**After (v3):**
> "...precisely what this framework is designed to **uncover**."

**Other Examples:**
- "demonstrates" → "suggests"
- "proves" → "illustrates"
- "exposes" → "reveals"
- Aggressive certainty → Measured confidence

**Purpose:** Maintains authority without sounding combative or arrogant. Creates collaborative tone even when presenting strong analysis.

---

### 7. Contextual Scaffolding

**Pattern:** Adds explicit positioning and necessary context to frame the work.

**Before (v1):**
> "So, how do we cut through all that? Well, I'm going to show you a dual-methodology approach..."

**After (v3):**
> "So, I've been thinking about how do we cut through all that? **In this research paper,** I'm going to show you a dual-methodology approach..."

**New Context Added (v3):**
> "Another factor was the 'hit' on production of the widely reported production in early 2025, which severely dented H1 production."

**Key Additions:**
- "In this piece of original research"
- "In this research paper"
- Specific production context for Valterra
- "(Stage 1)" precision markers
- Document type framing

**Purpose:** Helps reader understand document type, scope, and importance of specific details. Prevents misinterpretation and sets appropriate expectations.

---

### 8. Professional Protection

**Pattern:** Closes technical research with comprehensive risk warnings and formal attribution.

**Before (v1):**
> "As ever, do your own research.
>
> With my best wishes,
>
> Mick Pavey"

**After (v3):**
> "**Risk Warning:** Investing involves significant risks, including the potential loss of your entire investment. The value of investments can fluctuate, and past performance is not indicative of future results. This report does not constitute financial advice; you should carefully assess your financial situation, risk tolerance, and investment objectives before making decisions. Always conduct your own research (DYOR) and consult an independent financial advisor if necessary. This report is produced for informational purposes only and does not constitute a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any securities. As ever, do your own research (DYOR).
>
> **DYOR: Mick Pavey (16th February 2026)**"

**Key Changes:**
- Full legal disclaimer paragraph
- Formal date attribution
- DYOR emphasis (repeated)
- Professional risk language
- Regulatory compliance

**Purpose:** Protects both author and reader. Emphasizes personal responsibility (DYOR philosophy) while acknowledging regulatory requirements for investment research.

## Usage Guidelines by Content Type

### Research Papers (Full Deployment)
**Apply:** All 8 patterns strongly
**Examples:** PGM valuation methodology, sector analysis, new investment frameworks
**Tone:** Authoritative but personal, rigorous but accessible

### Newsletters (Selective Deployment)
**Apply Heavily:** Patterns 1-4 (Personal Attribution, Humility, Audience, Transitions)
**Apply Moderately:** Patterns 5-7 (Clarity, Softer Language, Context)
**Apply As Needed:** Pattern 8 (Professional Protection - brief version)
**Examples:** Weekly market updates, portfolio reviews, sector commentary

### Quick Updates (Minimal Deployment)
**Apply Primarily:** Patterns 3-4 (Audience Specificity, Conversational Transitions)
**Apply Sparingly:** Others as context demands
**Examples:** Breaking news, quick takes, brief announcements

### Substack Notes (Moderate Deployment)
**Apply Strongly:** Patterns 1, 2, 3, 4 (Personal, Humility, Audience, Transitions)
**Apply Minimally:** Patterns 5-8
**Examples:** Short-form social content, quick insights, engagement pieces

## Critical Differences: Mixed Voice vs. Generic AI Voice

| Element | Generic AI Voice | Mick's Mixed Voice |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Opening** | "Let's talk about valuing mining companies." | "Over the past few months, it's occurred to me that we need a different way..." |
| **Assertions** | "The framework demonstrates that..." | "To me (IMHO), the framework suggests that..." |
| **Audience** | "Investors who..." | "DIY-investors who..." |
| **Transitions** | "Right, let's conclude." | "OK, let me bring this all together." |
| **Technical terms** | "designed to pierce opacity" | "designed to uncover opacity" |
| **Certainty** | "The analysis proves..." | "The analysis illustrates..." |
| **Closing** | "Do your own research." | **Risk Warning:** [full disclaimer] + **DYOR: Mick Pavey (date)** |

## Implementation Checklist

When writing technical research in Mick's voice:

- [ ] **Opening:** Start with personal journey/thought process, not generic statement
- [ ] **Throughout:** Inject "(IMHO)", "To me,", "It seems obvious to me" into key assertions
- [ ] **Audience:** Use "diy-investors" not "investors"
- [ ] **Transitions:** Use "OK, let me" not "Right, let's"
- [ ] **Scope:** Add "example", "typically", explicit limitations
- [ ] **Language:** Choose "uncover/reveal/suggest" over "pierce/expose/prove"
- [ ] **Context:** Frame document type and add necessary background
- [ ] **Closing:** Include full risk warning + "DYOR: Mick Pavey (date)"

## Example Application: Transforming a Single Paragraph

**Generic AI Version:**
> "The analysis identifies Tharisa plc as the value leader in physical production leverage. For every £1 invested, shareholders acquire exposure to 0.000355 ounces of PGM production. This represents approximately 2.4 times more metal than Valterra Platinum. Investors should favor stocks with the highest physical production leverage during bull markets."

**Mick's Mixed Voice Version:**
> "The analysis identifies Tharisa plc as the undisputed leader in physical production leverage. For every £1 invested in Tharisa, the shareholder acquires a claim to 0.000355 ounces of PGM production—approximately 2.4 times more metal than an investment in Valterra Platinum. During a sector-wide rally driven by structural deficits (such as the current 2026-2030 platinum outlook), the mathematically superior strategy **(IMHO)** is to favour stocks with the highest physical production leverage. Of the three stocks analysed, Tharisa plc offers the greatest 'multiplier effect' on profit margins as prices rise above its fixed cost base."

**Changes Applied:**
- ✅ Audience specificity: "shareholders" → "the shareholder"
- ✅ Humility marker: "(IMHO)" added to strategy assertion
- ✅ Clarity: "Of the three stocks analysed" for explicit scope
- ✅ Softer language: "favor" → "favour" (British), measured tone
- ✅ Context: "current 2026-2030 platinum outlook" added
- ✅ Precision: "investment in" added for grammatical clarity
